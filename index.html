ECO-BOOST: Formulation and Effectiveness Assessment of an Organic Anti-Drought Fertilizier Tablet Using Vermicast (Eisenia Fetida) as Nutrient Base and Coco Peat as Moisture Retainer on Chili Plants (Capsicum Frutescens)

Vhilbrain Domingo, Celine Jalbuna, Eian Gabriel Morales, Rebekah Julienne Racelis

Abstract

This research investigated the effectiveness of the ECO-BOOST tablet, a biodegradable organic anti-drought fertilizer made from cocopeat and vermicast, in improving soil moisture retention and enhancing the growth of chili sprouts (Capsicum frutescens) under water-limited conditions. It specifically examined the effects of five treatments, including varying cocopeat–vermicast ratios and watering intervals, as well as conditions without the tablet, to determine which approach was most effective for early plant growth and drought survival. The study also evaluated plant height, number of leaves, and drought stress indicators such as leaf wilting, discoloration, and leaf drop. Using an experimental design conducted in a greenhouse over 50 days, the data were analyzed through One-Way Analysis of Variance (ANOVA) and Dunnett’s test. Results showed significant differences among treatments, with the 50% cocopeat–50% vermicast ECO-BOOST tablet under an 8-day watering interval producing the best growth performance and lowest drought stress symptoms. In contrast, treatments without the tablet showed poorer growth and higher stress levels, while leaf drop showed no significant difference. Overall, the findings highlight the ECO-BOOST tablet as a sustainable and effective solution for improving plant growth, moisture retention, and drought tolerance in agricultural practices. long run, this study contributes to sustainable agricultural practices by introducing an environmentally friendly soil additive that improves moisture retention, supports early plant development, and enhances drought tolerance in crops.

Introduction

Drought is a persistent environmental challenge that significantly affects agricultural productivity by reducing soil moisture, limiting nutrient availability, and impairing plant physiological processes. In drought-prone regions such as the Philippines, the need for sustainable and accessible solutions has become increasingly urgent. Conventional approaches often address either water scarcity or nutrient deficiency separately, creating a gap in integrated solutions that can simultaneously improve soil moisture retention and plant nutrition. In response to this issue, this study developed ECO-BOOST, a biodegradable organic anti-drought fertilizer tablet formulated from cocopeat and vermicast. Cocopeat functions as a moisture-retaining material, while vermicast serves as a nutrient-rich organic fertilizer. This research aimed to evaluate the effectiveness of ECO-BOOST in enhancing early vegetative growth, improving soil moisture retention, and increasing drought tolerance of chili plants (Capsicum frutescens). Specifically, it examined the effects of five treatments with varying cocopeat–vermicast ratios and watering intervals, measured plant height and number of leaves, assessed drought stress indicators such as leaf wilting, discoloration, and leaf drop, and determined whether significant differences existed among treatments using One-Way Analysis of Variance (ANOVA) at a 0.10 level of significance.

Methodology

This study employed an experimental research design conducted over a 50-day period in a controlled greenhouse environment to minimize external variability such as rainfall, temperature fluctuations, and wind exposure. Chili sprouts were grown in uniform wooden planter boxes to ensure consistency in soil volume and conditions. Five treatment groups were established: three groups received ECO-BOOST tablets with different cocopeat–vermicast ratios (50%–50%, 70%–30%, and 30%–70%) under an 8-day watering interval, while two control groups were maintained without the ECO-BOOST tablet—one with a 2-day watering interval and another with an 8-day watering interval. The ECO-BOOST tablets were formulated using organic materials and molded into biodegradable units designed to gradually release nutrients while retaining moisture in the soil. Data collection focused on key indicators of plant growth and drought response, including plant height, number of leaves, leaf wilting, leaf discoloration, and leaf drop. These variables were systematically observed and recorded throughout the experimental period. Statistical analysis was performed using One-Way ANOVA to identify significant differences among treatments, followed by Dunnett’s test to compare each treatment against the control groups.

Results

The results of the study revealed statistically significant differences among the five treatments in terms of plant height, number of leaves, leaf wilting, and leaf discoloration. Among all experimental groups, the treatment using the ECO-BOOST tablet composed of 50% cocopeat and 50% vermicast under an 8-day watering interval demonstrated the highest overall growth performance. Plants in this group exhibited greater height, increased leaf production, and reduced signs of drought stress compared to other treatments. Additionally, this formulation showed the lowest levels of leaf wilting and discoloration, indicating improved moisture retention and plant health under limited watering conditions. In contrast, the control groups without the ECO-BOOST tablet showed significantly lower growth performance and higher stress responses, particularly under the extended 8-day watering interval. However, the variable of leaf drop did not show a statistically significant difference across treatments, suggesting that this indicator may be less sensitive to the effects of the ECO-BOOST formulation within the duration of the study.

Discussion

The findings of this study demonstrate that the ECO-BOOST tablet is an effective solution for enhancing plant growth and drought tolerance through the combined effects of moisture retention and nutrient availability. The superior performance of the 50% cocopeat–50% vermicast formulation highlights the importance of achieving a balanced composition between water-holding capacity and nutrient supply. Cocopeat contributed to prolonged soil moisture retention, ensuring continuous water availability to plant roots, while vermicast provided essential macro- and micronutrients that supported plant development. The integration of these components into a single biodegradable tablet allowed for a sustained release of both water and nutrients, helping plants maintain physiological processes such as photosynthesis and nutrient absorption under water-limited conditions. The lack of significant difference in leaf drop may be attributed to the relatively short duration of the experiment or the nature of leaf drop as a later-stage stress response. Overall, the study supports the concept that combining organic moisture-retaining materials with natural fertilizers can significantly improve plant resilience to drought. ECO-BOOST presents a practical, cost-effective, and environmentally sustainable innovation that can benefit farmers, particularly in drought-prone areas, by reducing water dependency, enhancing crop performance, and promoting sustainable agricultural practices.
