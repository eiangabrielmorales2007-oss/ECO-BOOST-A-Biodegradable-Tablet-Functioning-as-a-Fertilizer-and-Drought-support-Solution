ECO-BOOST: Formulation and Effectiveness Assessment of an Organic Anti-Drought Fertilizier Tablet Using Vermicast (Eisenia Fetida) as Nutrient Base and Coco Peat as Moisture Retainer on Chili Plants (Capsicum Frutescens)

Vhilbrain Domingo, Celine Jalbuna, Eian Gabriel Morales, Rebekah Julienne Racelis

Abstract

This research investigated the effectiveness of the ECO-BOOST tablet, a biodegradable organic anti-drought fertilizer made from cocopeat and vermicast, in improving soil moisture retention and enhancing the growth of chili sprouts (Capsicum frutescens) under water-limited conditions. It specifically examined the effects of five treatments, including varying cocopeat–vermicast ratios and watering intervals, as well as conditions without the tablet, to determine which approach was most effective for early plant growth and drought survival. The study also evaluated plant height, number of leaves, and drought stress indicators such as leaf wilting, discoloration, and leaf drop. Using an experimental design conducted in a greenhouse over 50 days, the data were analyzed through One-Way Analysis of Variance (ANOVA) and Dunnett’s test. Results showed significant differences among treatments, with the 50% cocopeat–50% vermicast ECO-BOOST tablet under an 8-day watering interval producing the best growth performance and lowest drought stress symptoms. In contrast, treatments without the tablet showed poorer growth and higher stress levels, while leaf drop showed no significant difference. Overall, the findings highlight the ECO-BOOST tablet as a sustainable and effective solution for improving plant growth, moisture retention, and drought tolerance in agricultural practices. long run, this study contributes to sustainable agricultural practices by introducing an environmentally friendly soil additive that improves moisture retention, supports early plant development, and enhances drought tolerance in crops.

Introduction

Drought is one of the most significant environmental challenges affecting agricultural productivity across the globe. It is characterized by prolonged periods of insufficient rainfall, resulting in reduced soil moisture, limited water availability, and impaired plant growth. Beyond its environmental impact, drought also creates serious socioeconomic consequences, including decreased crop yield, food insecurity, and financial strain on farming communities. In the Philippines, this issue is further intensified by climate change, rising temperatures, and irregular rainfall patterns, making agriculture increasingly vulnerable to water scarcity. One of the primary effects of drought is the disruption of plant physiological processes, such as photosynthesis and nutrient absorption, which leads to stunted growth, wilting, and eventual plant death. Although several technologies have been developed to address drought, including irrigation systems and drought-resistant crops, many of these solutions remain costly and inaccessible to small-scale farmers. Furthermore, most existing approaches treat moisture retention and fertilization as separate concerns, limiting their overall effectiveness under drought conditions.

In response to these challenges, this study introduces ECO-BOOST, an organic anti-drought fertilizer tablet designed to improve both soil moisture retention and nutrient availability. The product is formulated using biodegradable and sustainable materials such as coco peat, vermicast, cassava starch hydrogel, and aloe vera gel. Coco peat functions as a moisture-retaining medium capable of holding significant amounts of water, while vermicast serves as a nutrient-rich organic fertilizer that enhances plant growth. Additional materials such as aloe vera gel and cassava starch hydrogel contribute to water retention and gradual nutrient release, creating a more stable growing environment for plants. This study focuses on chili plants (Capsicum frutescens) due to their economic importance and sensitivity to water stress, making them suitable for evaluating drought-related responses. The primary objective of this research is to determine the effectiveness of ECO-BOOST in enhancing early plant growth, improving soil moisture retention, and increasing drought tolerance. Specifically, the study aims to evaluate plant height, number of leaves, and visible stress indicators such as wilting, discoloration, and leaf drop, as well as to determine whether there are statistically significant differences among treatments.

Methodology

This study employed an experimental research design to evaluate the effectiveness of the ECO-BOOST tablet in promoting plant growth and drought resilience. The experiment was conducted over a period of fifty days in a controlled greenhouse environment to ensure uniformity in external conditions such as temperature, humidity, and light exposure. Chili plants (Capsicum frutescens) were used as the experimental subjects and were grown in wooden planter boxes to maintain consistency in soil volume and structure. The study involved five treatment groups, each subjected to different combinations of ECO-BOOST composition and watering intervals. These treatments included varying ratios of coco peat and vermicast (50:50, 70:30, and 30:70) under an eight-day watering interval, as well as control groups without ECO-BOOST under both frequent (two-day) and restricted (eight-day) watering conditions.

The ECO-BOOST tablet was formulated using a combination of organic materials, including coco peat, vermicast, cassava starch hydrogel, aloe vera gel, banana peel powder, eggshell powder, charcoal dust, and water. These materials were carefully mixed to create a uniform composition, molded into tablet form, and dried before application. The tablets were then placed into the soil where the chili seeds were planted. Throughout the experimental period, the plants were regularly monitored, and data were systematically collected. The variables measured in the study included plant height and number of leaves as indicators of vegetative growth, as well as leaf wilting, discoloration, and leaf drop as indicators of drought stress.

To analyze the collected data, statistical methods were applied, specifically One-Way Analysis of Variance (ANOVA) to determine whether significant differences existed among the treatment groups. Additionally. The level of significance was set at 0.10 to evaluate whether the observed differences in plant growth and stress response were statistically meaningful.

Results

The results of the study revealed that the application of the ECO-BOOST tablet had a significant effect on the growth performance and drought tolerance of chili plants. Among the different treatment groups, the formulation consisting of 50% coco peat and 50% vermicast under an eight-day watering interval demonstrated the highest effectiveness. Plants in this group exhibited the greatest increase in height and produced a higher number of leaves compared to other treatments, indicating improved vegetative growth. In addition, these plants showed reduced signs of drought stress, such as minimal leaf wilting and discoloration, suggesting that the ECO-BOOST tablet effectively enhanced soil moisture retention and nutrient availability.

In contrast, plants that were not treated with ECO-BOOST, particularly those subjected to extended watering intervals, exhibited lower growth performance and more severe stress indicators. These plants showed reduced height, fewer leaves, and increased levels of wilting and discoloration, indicating that the absence of the tablet negatively affected their ability to cope with water scarcity. While leaf drop was observed across treatments, the differences were not statistically significant, suggesting that this parameter may be less sensitive as an indicator of drought stress during early plant development.

The statistical analysis further supported these observations. The results of the ANOVA test indicated that there were significant differences among the treatment groups in terms of plant height, number of leaves, leaf wilting, and leaf discoloration. Consequently, the null hypothesis was rejected, and the alternative hypothesis was accepted, confirming that the ECO-BOOST tablet had a significant effect on plant growth and drought response.

Discussion

The findings of this study highlight the effectiveness of the ECO-BOOST tablet as an innovative solution for improving plant growth and drought tolerance. The superior performance of the 50:50 coco peat and vermicast formulation suggests that a balanced combination of moisture retention and nutrient supply is essential for optimal plant development under water-limited conditions. Coco peat contributed to the retention of soil moisture by maintaining water availability in the root zone, while vermicast provided essential nutrients and supported microbial activity, enhancing overall plant health. The integration of these components allowed the ECO-BOOST tablet to address both water scarcity and nutrient deficiency simultaneously, which is a limitation in many conventional agricultural practices.

The results also support existing theories in soil science and plant physiology, particularly the importance of maintaining a stable soil–plant–atmosphere continuum and ensuring continuous water and nutrient availability for plant growth. By improving soil structure and prolonging moisture retention, ECO-BOOST helped sustain physiological processes such as photosynthesis and nutrient transport, even under restricted watering conditions. This contributed to reduced stress symptoms and improved plant resilience. Furthermore, the use of biodegradable and locally available materials makes the ECO-BOOST tablet a sustainable and cost-effective alternative to synthetic fertilizers and irrigation systems.

However, the study is limited to early vegetative growth and was conducted under controlled greenhouse conditions, which may not fully represent real-world agricultural environments. Future research may explore long-term effects, different crop species, and field-based applications to further validate the effectiveness of ECO-BOOST. Despite these limitations, the study provides strong evidence that integrating moisture-retaining and nutrient-providing materials into a single product can significantly enhance plant performance under drought conditions. Overall, ECO-BOOST demonstrates potential as a practical, eco-friendly innovation that supports sustainable agriculture and helps farmers adapt to increasing water scarcity.
